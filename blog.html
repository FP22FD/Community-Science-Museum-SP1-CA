Children's curiosity

What is its importance in learning and how to stimulate it?

Through children's curiosity there is a development of creativity and reasoning of the brain. People are curious by nature, and children want to learn and know about things from an early age. There are different ways it manifests itself in the little ones, such as in questions, in games, in experiences, and in errors and successes. To encourage children to be curious and discover more, it is important to understand that curiosity is a natural process.

Generally, this characteristic is observed in the first year of life as an internal process of the child, without any external manifestations. A child's perception of the world around him begins to emerge in the second year of life, as well as his awareness that people and things are different from him. This is when the famous "why phase" begins.
