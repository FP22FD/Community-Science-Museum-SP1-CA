Children's curiosity: what is its importance in learning and how to stimulate it?

What is its importance in learning and how to stimulate it? Through the curiosity of children there is a development of creativity and reasoning of the brain. Therefore, when curiosity is cultivated in a child, learning can extend beyond the classroom. And this is not difficult to understand when we remember our own experiences. Whenever we are adults, and something interests us, we are able to memorize and learn a lot of information very quickly. This is because we are very interested in it. Consequently, it makes us want to learn more and more about the argument. Through children's curiosity there is a development of creativity and reasoning of the brain. People are curious by nature, and children want to learn and know about things from an early age. There are different ways it manifests itself in the little ones, such as in questions, in games, in experiences, and in errors and successes. To encourage children to be curious and discover more, it is important to understand that curiosity is a natural process. Generally, this characteristic is observed in the first year of life as an internal process of the child, without any external manifestations. A child's perception of the world around him begins to emerge in the second year of life, as well as his awareness that people and things are different from him. This is when the famous "why phase" begins. Here at the Community Science Museum we have many activities that stimulate children's creativity and curiosity. When you bring your children to our center, you will be amazed by how much they learn and enjoy themselves at the same time.
